Name: Leatherback Turtle
Scientific Name: Dermochelys coriacea

Mesoamerican Reef, Coastal East Africa, Gulf of California, The Galápagos, Coral Triangle

While the number of remaining turtles is still significant and their population is disperesed, they have faced a serious decline over the last century. This is a result of intense egg collection and the bycatch of fisheries. The species is currently listed as a vulnerable classification, however many subpopulations can be considered critically endangered. The leatherbacks are the largestest of the sea turtle species as well as the most migratory, crossing both the Pacific and the Atlantic Ocean. They range from 55-63 inches in length and weigh anywhere between 600-1500 pounds.

Name: Mountain Gorilla
Scientific Name: Gorilla beringei beringei

Mountain gorillas have a significantly thicker fur than any other species of ape, allowing them to survive in conditions below freezing.
